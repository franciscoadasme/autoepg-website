Meet the awesome folks who make AutoEPG

  • Francisco is a Bioinformatic Engineer and a PhD student in Applied Sciences at Universidad de Talca, Chile. He is the developer behind AutoEPG and has developed some routines in other science fields.

    His main research interests are related to the study of molecular interactions in several protein-drug systems through computational chemistry methods, but he also serves as web developer and recently, Mac OS X developer.

    about.me/franciscoadasme

  • Camila proposed AutoEPG's development during her internship at Dr. Ramirez's laboratory back in 2011. Since then, she has taken the lead of the project as well as she took care of product development and testing.

    She belongs to the Centre for Bioinformatics and Molecular Simulations (CBSM), where she's currently conducting his PhD thesis on the molecular study of A2A receptor inhibitors.

    about.me/camila.munoz

  • Claudio is interested on insect-plant interactions emphasizing proximal (ecological) and distal (evolutionary) causes. Particulary, I have been studying behavioural and morphological mechanisms underlying insect-feeding patterns, as well as plant responses to insects herbivory.

    I have studied some of these topics working with cereal aphids (Hemiptera:Aphididae), which are important agronomical pest in Chile.

    biologia.utalca.cl
